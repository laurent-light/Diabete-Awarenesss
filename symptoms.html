Recognizing the Symptoms of Diabetes

Diabetes can cause a variety of symptoms, some of which may be mild and others more severe. Recognizing these signs early can help with timely diagnosis and management.

Frequent Urination

People with diabetes may urinate more often due to excess glucose pulling water from body tissues.

Increased Thirst

Excess urination leads to dehydration, causing extreme thirst.

Unexplained Weight Loss

When the body can't use glucose for energy, it starts burning fat and muscle instead, leading to weight loss.

Extreme Hunger

Without proper insulin function, the body feels constantly hungry even after eating.

Slow Healing Wounds

High blood sugar levels can affect blood flow and immune function, causing wounds to heal slowly.

Blurred Vision

Fluctuations in blood sugar levels can cause fluid shifts in the eyes, leading to temporary blurred vision.

Fatigue and Weakness

Cells struggling to access glucose for energy can lead to persistent tiredness.
